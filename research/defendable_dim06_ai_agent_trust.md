## Framework Summary: Key Standards and Requirements

### 1. NIST AI RMF Agentic Profile (May 2026)

The Cloud Security Alliance published the NIST AI RMF Agentic Profile, introducing comprehensive extensions to the NIST AI Risk Management Framework specifically for agentic AI systems [^13^].

**Four-Tier Autonomy Classification:**
- **Tier 1 - Fully Supervised:** Outputs require human approval before action
- **Tier 2 - Constrained Autonomy:** Pre-approved action types within defined scope; human escalation for out-of-scope actions
- **Tier 3 - Broad Autonomy:** Autonomous action within defined boundaries; continuous monitoring; hard constraints on resources and scope
- **Tier 4 - Full Autonomy:** Capable of spawning sub-agents, acquiring tools, long-horizon plans; oversight board review required

**Key Governance Requirements:**
- **AG-GV.1:** Autonomy Tier Classification policy required for every deployed agent
- **AG-GV.2:** Delegation Accountability framework with formal oversight boundaries and accountability lineage connecting every agent action to a responsible human officer
- **AG-GV.3:** Agent Inventory and Lifecycle governance tracking authorities, tools, delegation relationships, and review/revocation schedules
- **AG-MP.1:** Tool-Use Risk Inventory classifying tools by consequence scope, reversibility, authentication requirements, and compositional risk
- **AG-MS.1:** Runtime Behavioral Telemetry required for Tier 2+ (action velocity, permission escalation rate, cross-boundary invocations, delegation depth, exception rates)

### 2. EU AI Act (Operational 2024-2026)

The EU AI Act entered force August 1, 2024, with phased implementation [^220^]:
- **February 2, 2025:** Prohibited AI practices and AI literacy obligations
- **August 2, 2025:** GPAI model obligations; enforcement powers begin August 2026
- **August 2, 2026:** Full applicability for high-risk AI systems
- **Penalties:** Up to EUR 35 million or 7% global annual turnover

**Key Prohibitions:**
- Biometric categorization systems based on sensitive characteristics
- Untargeted scraping of facial images for recognition databases
- Emotion recognition in workplace and schools
- Social scoring
- Predictive policing based solely on profiling
- AI that manipulates human behavior or exploits vulnerabilities

### 3. ISO/IEC 42001 - AI Management System (AIMS)

Published in late 2023, ISO/IEC 42001 is the world's first certifiable international standard for AI management systems [^228^][^229^][^230^].

**Key Requirements:**
- Establish AI Management System (AIMS) with defined governance structures
- AI risk management including bias, accountability, and data protection
- Ethical AI principles (transparency, fairness, accountability)
- Continuous monitoring and improvement
- Stakeholder engagement across compliance, development, and risk teams
- Plan-Do-Check-Act (PDCA) operational model
- Three-year certification cycle with annual surveillance

### 5. OECD AI Incidents and Hazards Monitor (AIM)

The OECD operates the AI Incidents Monitor, tracking media-reported AI incidents across 14 themes [^231^][^233^]. Key trends:

- **Cyberattacks and fraud:** Increased 2.7x as share of total reports (3.7% in 2022 to ~10% in 2024-2025)
- **Synthetic media/deepfakes:** Grew 2.5x between 2022 and 2025, representing 14%+ of total in Q3 2025
- **Child safety incidents:** Doubled between 2022 and 2025
- **AI-enabled warfare:** Steady increase since January 2024, averaging 12.3% of reports
- **Autonomous vehicles:** Decreased 55% from 2022, but still significant
- **OECD Common Reporting Framework:** 88 criteria across 8 dimensions for standardized incident reporting

## Incident Registry: Documented Cases (Chronological)

### INC-001: Air Canada Chatbot Bereavement Fare Hallucination (2024)
- **Offense Signal:** Fabrication / Unsupported Action
- **Description:** Air Canada's chatbot incorrectly told passenger Jake Moffatt he could book at full price and apply for a bereavement discount retroactively within 90 days. Air Canada denied the claim, citing actual policy prohibiting retroactive applications [^242^].
- **Pain Receipt:** Legal liability; British Columbia Civil Resolution Tribunal ordered Air Canada to pay C$812.02 in damages. Tribunal rejected Air Canada's defense that the chatbot was a "separate legal entity"
- **Defense Fixer Required:** Output verification, human review gates, policy consistency checking
- **Source Tier:** T2 (Legal/Industry Analysis)

### INC-002: Walters v. OpenAI - Defamation Hallucination (June 2023 - May 2025)
- **Offense Signal:** Fabrication (Hallucination)
- **Description:** ChatGPT falsely stated that radio host Mark Walters had embezzled funds from the Second Amendment Foundation. Journalist Frederick Riehl (who was on SAF's board) immediately recognized the fabrication and never published it. Court dismissed defamation claim on May 19, 2025, ruling no reasonable person would take chatbot output as fact given disclaimers [^255^][^278^][^279^]
- **Pain Receipt:** Legal costs; reputational exposure; first-of-kind defamation litigation against AI company
- **Defense Fixer Required:** Output verification, provenance tracking, hallucination warnings
- **Source Tier:** T2 (Legal Practice)

### INC-003: Mobley v. Workday - AI Discrimination in Hiring (2023-2025)
- **Offense Signal:** Improper Data Handling / Unsupported Action
- **Description:** Derek Mobley, a Black disabled man over age 40, applied to 100+ jobs using Workday's AI screening platform and was rejected every time, sometimes within minutes at 1:50 AM. Court allowed claims to proceed under theory that Workday acted as "agent" of employers under Title VII, ADEA, and ADA [^258^][^260^][^265^][^269^]
- **Pain Receipt:** Nationwide class action certified May 2025 covering potentially millions of applicants over age 40; Workday represented that 1.1 billion applications were rejected using its tools during the relevant period
- **Defense Fixer Required:** Algorithmic auditing, bias testing, human review of AI decisions, audit logging
- **Source Tier:** T2 (Academic Legal / Legal Practice)

### INC-004: OpenClaw (ClawBot/MoltBot) Agent Security Crisis (2024-2026)
- **Offense Signal:** Improper Data Handling / Record Alteration / Missing Receipts
- **Description:** Open-source personal AI assistant with 100,000+ installations suffered multiple critical CVEs including CVE-2025-53773 (CVSS 9.6) for command injection. Ran with high-level privileges, plaintext API keys, no built-in authentication. Shodan scans found hundreds of exposed instances with no authentication [^222^][^245^]
- **Pain Receipt:** Complete system compromise; credential theft; active cryptocurrency theft campaigns exploiting prompt injection; 22% of enterprises had employees using it as shadow AI
- **Defense Fixer Required:** Agent sandboxing, least-privilege enforcement, authentication, behavioral telemetry, agent decommissioning procedures
- **Source Tier:** T2 (Security Research)

### INC-005: Moltbook Database Exposure - Agent Social Network (February 2026)
- **Offense Signal:** Improper Data Handling / Missing Receipts
- **Description:** AI agent social network platform with 1.5 million autonomous AI agents and 17,000 human operators (88:1 ratio). Database misconfiguration exposed credentials, API keys, and agent data. 506 prompt injections identified spreading through agent network [^222^][^245^]
- **Pain Receipt:** Massive credential exposure; prompt injection propagation through networked agents; capability theft incidents
- **Defense Fixer Required:** Agent inventory governance, database security, prompt injection detection, network isolation
- **Source Tier:** T2 (Security Research)

## Taxonomy Mapping: Offense Signals -> Pain Receipts -> Defense Fixers

### OFFENSE SIGNALS

#### OS-01: Unsupported Action
- **Definition:** AI agent takes action without proper authorization, human approval, or within defined scope
- **Mapped Incidents:**
  - INC-001: Air Canada chatbot provided incorrect policy information
  - INC-009: Comet made purchases from fake storefronts
  - INC-012: Cruise robotaxi autonomous decision failure
  - INC-019: Runaway cost explosions from unbounded agent actions
  - INC-003: Workday AI auto-rejected job applicants within minutes
- **Pain Receipts:** Financial loss, legal liability, regulatory suspension, operational disruption, discriminatory outcomes
- **Defense Fixers:** Tier-based autonomy governance (NIST), human-in-the-loop gates, hard iteration limits, real-time cost monitoring, behavioral telemetry

#### OS-02: Fabrication (Hallucination)
- **Definition:** AI generates false, non-existent, or misleading information presented as factual
- **Mapped Incidents:**
  - INC-001: Air Canada chatbot fabricated bereavement policy
  - INC-002: ChatGPT fabricated embezzlement claims about Mark Walters
  - INC-013: AI hallucinated legal citations in court filings
  - INC-014: LLMs hallucinated 17-88% of legal citations
  - INC-015: Deepfakes fabricated celebrity endorsements for fraud
- **Pain Receipts:** Legal sanctions ($10K+), defamation litigation, malpractice risk, case dismissal, financial fraud ($1.28B+)
- **Defense Fixers:** Output verification, citation validation, retrieval-augmented generation, human expert review, provenance tracking

#### OS-03: Record Alteration / Memory Poisoning
- **Definition:** Agent memory or records corrupted with harmful instructions that persist across sessions
- **Mapped Incidents:**
  - INC-011: OpenAI Atlas memory poisoning via CSRF
  - INC-020: Silent quality degradation via cascading hallucinations
  - INC-006: CometJacking via poisoned URL parameters
- **Pain Receipts:** Persistent malicious behavior, cross-session compromise, undetected data corruption
- **Defense Fixers:** Memory poisoning detection, session isolation, memory integrity validation, behavioral drift monitoring

#### OS-04: Improper Data Handling
- **Definition:** Mishandling of sensitive data, credentials, or personal information
- **Mapped Incidents:**
  - INC-004: OpenClaw plaintext API keys, no authentication
  - INC-005: Moltbook database misconfiguration exposed credentials
  - INC-021: Zero-interaction data exfiltration
  - INC-025: Ross Intelligence used proprietary training data
- **Pain Receipts:** Credential theft, system compromise, $850K+ fraud losses, IP infringement liability
- **Defense Fixers:** Encryption, authentication, least-privilege access, database security, data provenance

#### OS-05: Missing Receipts / Accountability Gaps
- **Definition:** Inability to trace AI decisions to responsible parties or reconstruct decision chains
- **Mapped Incidents:**
  - INC-003: No accountability for Workday AI screening decisions
  - INC-004: OpenClaw lacked audit logging
  - INC-024: 88% of AI vendors cap liability in contracts
  - INC-012: Cruise failed to provide full picture to regulators
- **Pain Receipts:** Regulatory penalties, class action lawsuits, inability to demonstrate compliance
- **Defense Fixers:** Accountability lineage registers, agent action logging, audit trails, human officer assignment

#### OS-06: Unsafe Advice
- **Definition:** AI provides recommendations that could cause harm if followed
- **Mapped Incidents:**
  - INC-009: Comet clicked phishing links on behalf of users
  - INC-001: Air Canada chatbot gave incorrect travel advice
  - INC-008: Comet tricked into accessing Gmail, triggering account recovery
- **Pain Receipts:** Financial fraud, account compromise, identity theft
- **Defense Fixers:** Safety guardrails, output validation, human approval for consequential actions

## Emerging Themes and Cross-Cutting Observations

### Theme 1: The Accountability Gap
The single most significant finding across all sources is the accountability gap in AI deployment. While 88% of AI vendors cap liability and only 17% offer regulatory compliance warranties [^241^], courts are increasingly holding deployers responsible. The Mobley v. Workday precedent establishes AI vendors as "agents" of employers [^269^], while the Air Canada case makes clear that companies cannot outsource liability to AI vendors [^242^].

### Theme 2: The Hallucination Crisis
Hallucination is not a theoretical concern - it is a documented, accelerating crisis. In legal settings, cases rose from 10 (2023) to 73+ (first 5 months 2025) [^238^]. Stanford research shows even purpose-built legal AI tools hallucinate 17-34% of the time [^259^]. The Walters v. OpenAI case [^255^] shows current legal standards may protect AI companies (disclaimers, no actual damages), but this is likely to evolve as reliance increases.

### Theme 3: The Agentic Security Perimeter Collapse
Traditional security perimeters do not work for agentic AI. The OpenClaw/Moltbook incidents [^245^] show how local agents with system access become attack vectors. Browser agents (Perplexity Comet) [^252^][^253^] demonstrate that the entire concept of agentic browsers may be "fatally flawed" [^256^] because LLMs cannot distinguish trusted instructions from untrusted content.

### Theme 4: Synthetic Media as Trust Destruction
Deepfakes represent not just fraud but a fundamental threat to trust in digital media. With 900% annual growth, $1.28B in documented losses, and the ability to create convincing fakes for under $50 [^261^][^264^], the defensive response (C2PA provenance [^276^], detection systems) is racing to catch up.

### Theme 5: Standards Convergence
There is strong convergence across frameworks: NIST AI RMF Agentic Profile [^13^], ISO 42001 [^229^], EU AI Act [^220^], and OWASP [^274^] all emphasize governance structures, risk-based approaches, human oversight, audit trails, and accountability assignment. The DefendableOS taxonomy aligns directly with these emerging global standards.
